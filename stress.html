Stress

Stress is the body's response to perceived threats or challenges, triggering a cascade of physical, emotional, and behavioral reactions. It activates the "fight or flight" response, releasing hormones like adrenaline and cortisol. Stress can be acute, from short-term events like deadlines or arguments, or chronic, from ongoing issues like financial problems or work pressure. Causes of stress include life changes, such as moving or losing a job, and everyday pressures like family responsibilities. Psychological factors, such as anxiety or perfectionism, also contribute. Environmental factors, including noise or overcrowding, can heighten stress levels. Chronic stress can lead to health problems like hypertension, heart disease, and mental health disorders. Effective stress management includes relaxation techniques, physical activity, and seeking social support.

Solutions

  • Break study sessions into manageable chunks with regular breaks.
  • Practice deep breathing or meditation to relax your mind before and after studying.
  • Create a structured study schedule to stay organized and avoid last-minute cramming.
  • Engage in physical exercise daily to reduce tension and improve concentration.
  • Seek support from teachers, friends, or study groups when feeling overwhelmed.
